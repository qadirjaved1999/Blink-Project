Rights of a Customers

No matter where you’re going, you have a right to get there safely.
Your Captain should follow speed limits and obey the law during your whole trip, and they should be focused on the road.
To be eligible to drive for Blink, your driver must be at least XX year old and have been driving for at least XX years with a clean driving record, so you won’t end up with a reckless driver who doesn’t know how to drive.
Plus, your Blink Captain will have gone through a background check for any criminal offenses to avoid having a dangerous driver on the road.
Blink drivers must meet all requirements for auto insurance, so in the event of an accident, they will have coverage.
Any type of in-appropriate behavior from your driver is unacceptable. The Blink customer safety policy forbids .
Drivers are discouraged from commenting on their passengers’ appearance or relationship status.
If your Blink Captain says or does anything to make you feel uncomfortable, report them.
A safe and clean vehicle is one of your rights as a customer.
To be eligible for customer support, the vehicle must be years old or newer.
Blink plans on leasing cars to drivers who don’t own a vehicle in our next phase.

Also, a car that doesn’t look like the vehicle as shown in the app may be an imposter.

You have the right to report an accident or unsafe behavior that occurred during your ride.
You can report your experiences directly to Blink, or, depending on the severity of the incident, to the police.
