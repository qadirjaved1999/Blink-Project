Blink – Welcome to Enablement

Blink is a multi-vertical aggregator platform leveraging fragmentation in the travel and transportation space.

About Us

Blink is a ‘Software as a Service’ technology in the Mobility (cab hailing and car hire) and Transportation business. It provides a multi-vertical B2B2C marketplace to its Customers and Users via a mobile application.

Blink leverages pricing inefficiencies in Mobility, Road Freight Transportation, Air Cargo and Leisure Travel with the purpose of offering its users/customers competitive pricing on services. Real time price comparison within the app provides users the option of choosing the most cost efficient service.

Why Blink?

  • Blink is a complete eco-system designed to fulfill 'end to end' User and Customer requirements.
  • Blink’s ‘low cost’ pricing algorithm presents its Users with a choice of available competitor pricing. The User can view a list of providers offering a similar service in real time.
  • It is a multi-vertical, B2B2C aggregator marketplace. It leverages the inconsistencies in the travel and transportation industry. The Customers and Users have a choice of selecting the least expensive and most efficient service available.
  • Blink guarantees that the drivers get paid on time, such that they are able to manage their cash flow effectively.

On Demand & Rent A Car

How It Works:

  • Blinks Saas based application uses geo-positioning to identify your location on a Blink Mobile application.
  • The customer then chooses the destination. The drivers within your location get a notification.
  • The customer then selects a driver/vehicle based on the proposed pricing to destination while being able to 'comparison shop' within the same app, based on vehicle, make, and model.
  • Blink does not dictate pricing due to its de-regulated pricing model.
  • The Driver would propose a price based on a standard, already flagged for the said distance by the App.
  • The customer pays via a credit card / cash at the end of the journey.
  • Blink revenue streams are a function of the applicable margin.

Important features include:
  • Engagement and disengagement locations are within prescribed limits.
  • The eco-system is based on a de-regulated pricing mechanism for suppliers and comparison shopping for customers.
  • Blink Drivers would publish daily currency rates for a 10 hourly engagement.
  • Blink would publish the fuel charge (Local currency/km) for that category in a given geography.
  • Blink customer would use the Blink App to request for the service.

Rent A Car:

  • Blink Customer would use the Blink app requesting for the service.
  • Customers choose driver and vehicle type
  • Customer can engage the driver/vehicle for upto 10 hrs
  • Driver charges its fixed daily charge for a 10 hour engagement
  • Fuel cost is a pass through - driver is compensated based on published fuel rate

Blink Policies

What do I do if a customer makes a mess in my vehicle / dirties the vehicle ?
There are a few tasks that you need to action so that you are back on the road as quickly as possible, are compensated as quickly as possible. Your first task is to call 'Blink Care'.
Explain to them what has happened. Blink Care needs to be informed within 60 minutes of the incident.
You will provide us with your Blink ID for verification. Before you start driving again,
you should provide the following;

1. Two or three clear pictures of the damage/mess;
2. Brief description of what happened, additional circumatsnaces as it applies;
3. Valid cleaning reciept.

How often to I need to take a rest between rides? Is this enforced?
If you have accumulated +12 hours of continuous driving time, we will put you enforced break for a min of 8 hours.

What is regarded as a complete trip ?
This will be counted from the point that the ride is accepted by you until its conclusion - either drop off or cancellation

Why does Blink insist that I take breaks ?
This is to ensure that any Captains associated with the Blink brand are aware of the safety on the road not only of the customer but also of their own.

What is the ride cancellation policy at Blink?
When the rider cancels, you as the Captain get a cancellation fee if :
1. You are enroute to the pick-up location;
2. It happens x min after you had accepted the ride;
3. You were enroute to the pick up location and it happened within Xmin from arrival at the pick up location. Alternatively, the Customer cancels after you have arrived at the pick up location; You arrived no more than 5 min after the estimated time of arrival shown on your app when you accepted the booking - you waited for at least Xmin after you reached the pick up location after tapping 'Reached Pick Up location'.
You will NOT get a cancellation fee if - the cancellation takes place within the first 3 minutes after your acceptance; you are running more than X min late to the pick up location; you reach the pick up location after X min after the original estimated time of arrival; Cancellation fee will vary by type of car and location.
